Amazon Wildlife

The Amazon rainforest is home to an incredibly diverse array of wildlife, including over 400 billion
individual trees that host thousands of species of plants, birds, insects, and mammals. Notable inhabitants include jaguars, sloths, pink river dolphins, and numerous species of monkeys and parrots. This biodiversity makes the Amazon one of the most vital and unique ecosystems on the planet.

The Amazon River Basin is home to the largest rainforest on Earth. The basin roughly the size of the 48 contiguous United States covers some 40% of the South American continent and includes parts of eight South American countries: Brazil, Bolivia, Peru, Ecuador, Colombia, Venezuela, Guyana, and Suriname, as well as French Guiana, a department of France.

The Amazon Basin contains the world's largest rainforest, which represents over 60% of the world's remaining rainforests. More than half this forest lies in Brazil.The 390 billion trees across the Amazon rainforest locks up massive amounts of carbon in their leaves, branches, and trunks.Annual floods replenish nutrients in floodplain areas used for agriculture.

Amazon parrots are intelligent, friendly, affectionate, and sociable birds with many striking wild type color variations. For these reasons, they are popular pet birds. However, it takes an experienced bird owner to handle the parrot personality and to provide the care a parrot requires. They are mandatorily social creatures, constantly in need of interaction and attention.

Monitor lizards have long necks, powerful tails and claws, and well-developed limbs. The adult length of extant species ranges from 20 cm (7.9 in) in some species such as Varanus sparnus, to over 3m (10 ft) in the case of the Komodo dragon, though the extinct megalania (Varanus priscus) may have reached lengths of more than 7m (23 ft). Most monitor species are terrestrial.

Amazon Rainforest Butterflies are definitely a favorite sight for tourists in the Amazon Rainforest. Often, you can see large and colorful butterflies flying over the rivers. Butterflies are so popular that they are often regarded as ‘honorary vertebrates’ by some conservationists. This means they can have a higher protection than other invertebrates like other insects and spiders.

Common characteristics of modern bears include large bodies with stocky legs long snouts small rounded ears shaggy hair plantigrade paws with five nonretractile claws and short tails. Bears use shelters such as caves and logs as their dens most species occupy their dens during the winter for a long period of hibernation. They have an excellent sense of smell.
